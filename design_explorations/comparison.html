Bomu — Design Direction Explorations

Four fresh looks for the same "What can I make?" screen, plus a type specimen. These are concept mockups (pictures), not code. Pick a direction and I'll rebuild it as real CSS in your templates.

How to read these: Nano Banana renders a picture of a UI, so text/spacing won't be pixel-perfect and won't drop into your app as-is. What matters is the feel — palette, type, card style, depth. Whatever you pick becomes the spec I hand-build in HTML/CSS.
Direction 1
Warm Speakeasy
Art-deco lounge. Espresso/oxblood, brass accents, leather cards, elegant serif headings, Edison-bulb glow.
Read: Most on-theme and characterful. Risk: leather/gold textures are hard to reproduce faithfully in CSS and can read "skeuomorphic/dated" if overdone. Best if you go flatter than the render.
Direction 2
Clean Modern
Bright, airy, off-white. Grey cards, one terracotta accent, crisp geometric sans. 2025 fintech-quality calm.
Read: Easiest to build faithfully and ages well. Biggest departure — it drops your dark identity entirely. Great if you want "clean and premium" over "moody."
Direction 3
Neon Nightlife
Near-black with magenta/cyan/violet glows, per-drink colour coding, luminous edges.
Read: High energy, very "app store screenshot." Glows are cheap in CSS (box-shadow). Risk: fatiguing for a utility you open nightly, and colour-coding needs a real rule to not feel random.
Direction 4
Premium Glassy
Indigo→plum gradient, frosted glass cards, soft bokeh, coral accent kept from your current brand.
Read: My pick for you. It's the closest evolution of your existing navy+coral, feels "slick and fluid" as asked, and glassmorphism is a few CSS lines (backdrop-filter). Keeps continuity while looking a tier more premium.

Typography specimen

Three real, free font pairings you can actually load from Google Fonts / Fontshare — not extracted from the image. Playfair Display + Inter (classic, elegant), Fraunces + General Sans (warm, characterful — pairs well with Speakeasy/Glassy), Clash Display + Satoshi (bold, modern — pairs with Neon/Clean). Right now you use system fonts only, so even just adding a display face for headings is a quick, big upgrade.
